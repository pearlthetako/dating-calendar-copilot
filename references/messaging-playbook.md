# Messaging Playbook

Use these heuristics when drafting dating messages.

## Openers

- Lead with something specific from the profile when possible.
- Make the opener easy to answer in one message.
- Avoid interview mode with stacked questions.

Examples:

- `You seem serious about finding the best dumplings in town. What is the current number one?`
- `I respect the commitment to hiking photos. Which trail actually earned the bragging rights?`

## Building momentum

- Mirror the other person's message length and tone.
- Add one small self-reveal when the chat feels flat.
- Escalate from banter to a date only after signs of reciprocity.

Signals that support asking them out:

- They ask questions back.
- They respond consistently and with some detail.
- They engage with playful framing or future-oriented talk.

## Asking for the date

- Keep the ask low pressure and concrete.
- Pair the idea with a short reason if it fits the flow.
- Offer 2 to 3 time options, then let them choose or counter.

Examples:

- `This is fun. Want to continue it over drinks this week? I could do Thursday after 7 or Sunday afternoon.`
- `You seem worth meeting in real life. Coffee this weekend? I am free Saturday late morning or Sunday around 3.`

## Scheduling tone

- Share only the options that matter.
- Leave space for them to suggest another time.
- Avoid making the message sound like a booking bot.

Example:

- `If neither works, no worries, feel free to throw out another time that suits you.`
